Editorial standards & process

Who we are

GamblingLawyers.com is an independent editorial and directory platform covering gambling law, licensing and regulatory compliance across every major regulated market. Our editorial team combines legal research, regulatory monitoring and industry analysis to produce content that is accurate, current and practically useful for operators, suppliers and their counsel.

How we research and write

Every article published on this site follows a structured editorial process. We monitor primary regulatory sources — official gazettes, regulator publications, enforcement notices and legislative databases — across the jurisdictions we cover. Our editorial team cross-references multiple primary sources before publishing, and articles are reviewed for legal accuracy and practical relevance before they go live.

Our editorial principles

Accuracy first. We cite specific regulatory instruments, enforcement actions and legislative provisions. Where a development is provisional or subject to consultation, we say so. We do not speculate or sensationalise.

Independence. Our editorial coverage is not influenced by commercial relationships. Law firms and lawyers listed on the directory have no editorial input into or advance sight of our published analysis.

Practical value. We write for an audience of practitioners — in-house counsel, compliance officers, regulatory lawyers and senior executives in the gambling industry. Every article aims to answer the question: what does this mean in practice for the people who need to act on it?

Transparency. We identify the regulatory sources behind every development we cover. Where we express an editorial view, we distinguish it clearly from factual reporting.

Corrections

If you believe any article on this site contains a factual error, please contact us at info@gamblinglawyers.com. We investigate all corrections promptly and update published articles where necessary, noting the date and nature of any material correction.

Contact the editorial team

For editorial enquiries, corrections or contributor proposals, email info@gamblinglawyers.com.
