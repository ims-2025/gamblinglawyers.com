### Voice rules
- Write like The Economist, not like a directory.
- No exclamation marks. No emoji. No "unlock", "supercharge", "seamless".
- Prefer concrete nouns over adjectives. "Licensing in Brazil" beats "amazing legal services".
- Neutral on politics and operators — we serve the market, not a side of it.
- International English. Avoid US-only or UK-only idioms in global copy.
- Short paragraphs. Editorial rhythm.

### Suggested palette (to brief the builder)
- Ink (#0B0F14) — primary dark
- Bone (#F5F1E8) — off-white backgrounds
- Oxford (#0F2A47) — deep navy accent
- Gilt (#B28A3E) — muted gold for highlights and badges
- Slate (#4A5260) — secondary text
Pair a serif display (e.g. GT Super, Canela, Tiempos Headline) with a clean neutral sans (e.g. Inter, Söhne, Neue Haas Grotesk).

## 4. About page

### Hero

**Eyebrow:** About GamblingLawyers.com
**Headline (serif):** A single reference point for gambling law, worldwide.
**Subhead:** GamblingLawyers.com is where the international gaming industry finds specialist legal counsel and the intelligence to navigate regulated markets.

### The platform
We built GamblingLawyers.com because the gaming industry was missing what other regulated sectors take for granted: a trusted, curated, global reference point for legal expertise.

Gambling is among the most heavily regulated industries in the world. Licensing regimes differ by country, state and province. Compliance obligations shift with every political cycle. Deals are scrutinised, enforcement is rising, and the cost of the wrong advisor is measured in revoked licences, frozen payments and lost market access.

Operators, investors and founders deserve a better starting point. So do the specialist lawyers who spend their careers inside this industry.

### What we do
GamblingLawyers.com operates as two connected products under one editorial standard:

A **curated directory** of law firms and individual lawyers with demonstrated specialisation in gambling, betting, casino, sports betting, sweepstakes, fantasy sports, fintech-for-gaming and the wider iGaming sector.

A **premium editorial desk** covering regulation, licensing, enforcement, M&A, payments, responsible gaming and market entry across the world's most important gaming jurisdictions.

### How introductions work
We do not publish phone numbers. We do not redirect inquiries to third parties. When you submit an inquiry through the platform, it reaches our desk. We review it, match it against the directory and introduce you to the advisor most suited to your jurisdiction, sector and stage.

This is how we protect the quality of the platform, the confidentiality of our users and the signal-to-noise ratio for the lawyers we list.

### Who we serve
Online casino and sportsbook operators. iGaming startups and founders. Payment providers, PSPs and card schemes working with regulated gaming. Software, platform and content suppliers. Affiliates, marketing groups and media networks. Investors, acquirers and corporate development teams. In-house legal and compliance functions. And specialist lawyers who want their expertise to reach the right clients.

### Our editorial standards
We review every profile against criteria for specialisation, experience and jurisdiction. We disclose featured and verified status clearly. We do not accept content that is pay-to-play disguised as editorial. Our news and analysis are independent of the directory, and corrections are issued publicly when errors are made.

A full statement of our editorial standards is available on request.

### Independence
GamblingLawyers.com is an independent directory and editorial platform. It is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. Nothing on the platform constitutes an endorsement, a ranking or a guarantee of outcome.

**Contact:** info@gamblinglawyers.com

### The case for being listed

**Headline:** Four reasons to be on GamblingLawyers.com

1. **Qualified inquiries, not tyre-kickers**
   Every inquiry is reviewed by our desk and matched to a jurisdiction and practice area before any introduction is made. You receive fewer, better briefs.

2. **Curated peer group**
   The directory is specialist-only. Being listed places you alongside the firms and lawyers the market already associates with gambling expertise.

4. **A global storefront**
   Operators, investors and founders from every major regulated market reach the platform through search, referrals and editorial traffic. Your profile is discoverable in the context where it matters.

- Demonstrated specialisation in gambling, betting, casino, sports betting, fantasy sports, sweepstakes, iGaming, fintech-for-gaming or an adjacent regulated vertical.

### FAQ strip (accordion)

**Is there a fee to be listed?**
Basic directory listings are free for qualifying specialists during the platform's launch phase. Featured placements, editorial amplification and premium profile options will be priced separately and disclosed clearly on the site.

**Will clients contact me directly?**
No. All inquiries reach the GamblingLawyers.com desk first. We review them and make introductions personally, with your agreement. Direct routing may be offered as an option in future.

**Can I edit my own profile?**
Yes. Once your profile is live you can submit updates to our team, and a self-service editor is on the roadmap.

**How are featured profiles chosen?**
Featured placements are selected by our editorial team on the basis of relevance, activity, specialisation and — where applicable — paid featured inventory, which is always disclosed.

**Do you rank lawyers or firms?**
No. GamblingLawyers.com is a directory and editorial platform. We do not publish numerical rankings and do not sell rankings.
